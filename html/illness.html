Powdery Midlew

Powdery Mildew is a fungal disease that affects many plants such as grapes, cucumbers, roses, and squash. Symptoms: White, powder-like spots on leaves and stems. Yellowing, leaf curling, and reduced growth or yield.

Anthracnose

Anthracnose is a fungal disease that attacks many plants such as beans, cucumbers, tomatoes, grapes, and fruit trees. Symptoms: Dark brown to black spots on leaves, stems, and fruits. Fruit cracking or rotting. Premature leaf drop and overall weak growth.

Fire Blight

Fire Blight is a serious bacterial disease (caused by Erwinia amylovora) that mainly attacks apple, pear, and other members of the Rosaceae family. Symptoms: Blackened, wilted blossoms and shoots that look scorched or burned. Twigs and branches often bend into a “shepherd’s crook” shape. Cankers (sunken, dark lesions) on branches that may ooze a sticky, amber-colored fluid.

Soft Rot

Soft Rot is a bacterial disease, most often caused by Erwinia carotovora (now Pectobacterium spp.), that affects vegetables and fruits such as potatoes, carrots, cucumbers, onions, and many others, especially after harvest. Symptoms: Water-soaked, soft spots on tubers, roots, or fruits that quickly turn into brown or gray mush. Characteristic foul odor. Plant tissues collapse and break apart easily when touched.

Wheat Drawfing

Wheat Dwarf is a viral disease (commonly caused by Wheat Dwarf Virus – WDV) that primarily infects wheat and sometimes barley. It is mainly transmitted by leafhoppers. Symptoms: Stunted growth—plants remain short.

CMV

Cucumber Mosaic Virus (CMV) infects cucumbers, tomatoes, peppers, eggplants, many ornamentals, and various weeds. Symptoms: Light and dark green mottling (“mosaic”) on leaves. Leaf distortion, curling, and reduced size.
