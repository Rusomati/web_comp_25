Fodder beet

Fodder beet (also called mangold or feed beet) is a type of beet grown specifically for animal feed rather than human consumption. Key Points: Purpose: Provides energy-rich feed for livestock such as cattle and sheep.

Quinoa

Quinoa is a gluten-free seed eaten like a grain. It’s rich in complete protein, fiber, and minerals, and grows well in dry or salty soil, making it a healthy, drought-tolerant crop used in salads, soups, and baking.

Coffe beans

Coffee beans are the seeds of the coffee tree’s fruit. They are harvested, dried, roasted, and ground to make coffee. The main types are Arabica, with a smooth flavor and mild acidity

Black Radish

Black radish is a type of radish distinguished by its dark skin and crisp white flesh. Quick facts: Nutritional value: Rich in vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants, supporting liver and digestive health.

Saffaron

Saffron is the world’s most expensive spice, obtained from the crimson stigmas of the purple Crocus sativus flower. Quick facts: Part used: The red stigmas are carefully dried and used as a spice and natural coloring.

Triticale

It is considered one of the rare crops in Jordan. Triticale is a hybrid cereal grain created by crossing wheat (Triticum) with rye (Secale). This cross combines the high yield and baking quality of wheat with the hardiness of rye, allowing the plant to thrive in poorer soils and withstand cold or drought.
